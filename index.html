Clinical · 2024

iRays

A device that reads the eye
before the hospital can.

Dual-spectrum pupillometry for prehospital neuro-triage. In the back of an ambulance — in the minutes that define survival — iRays reads what no unaided eye can see: the neurological signature of traumatic brain injury, encoded in the pupil's response to two frequencies of light simultaneously.

Patent pending. Built with the City of Hampton Fire & Rescue. Funded by NIH Mid-South REACH and the AI4Health Initiative.

The Vessel

A cathedral of patience
for a civilization leaving home.

On a launch pad in Florida, a rocket prepares to leave Earth. In a Norwegian forest, 1,000 spruce saplings planted in 2014 will not be harvested until 2114 — to become paper for books no one alive today will ever read. One is escape. One is permanence.

Dr. Yang leads an interdisciplinary Pathfinder asking: in an age of algorithmic production, what remains irreplaceably human? Her answer is rooted in Wu Wei — the stillness the machine cannot replicate. With collaborators across Physics, Art, English, Biology, Philosophy, and Libraries.
